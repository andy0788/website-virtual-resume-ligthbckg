Profile

From national traceability systems to social genetic effects.

I build statistical models that separate an animal's direct genetic effect from its social one — the part of its neighbors' performance that traces back to its own genes.

My dissertation applies this idea across commercial swine populations: feeding time and competition for feeder space captured by automatic feeders, maternal traits in group-housed gestating sows, and a genomic search for the regions that drive both, in collaboration with PIC and Topigs Norsvin.

I grew up in Tegucigalpa, Honduras, and trained as an agronomist at Zamorano University under its "Learn by Doing" philosophy. After directing Honduras's National Livestock Traceability System at SENASA, I came to the University of Florida for a master's in animal genetics with Dr. Raluca Mateescu.

Along the way I've mentored international students through the Zamorano alumni network and kept a small Substack on science and writing.

Dissertation

Three ways of asking the same question.

01

Follower effects on feeder occupation in finishing pigs

Using automatic feeder data, I model how the pig that feeds just before you — the one you "follow" — leaves a genetic fingerprint on your own feeding time and competition for feeder space.

02

Social genetic effects on maternal traits in group-housed gestating sows

Group housing reshapes gestation for sows. I estimate the direct and social genetic components of maternal traits, testing whether selection that ignores pen-mates leaves welfare and productivity on the table.

03

Single-step GWAS for direct and social genetic effects

A proposed single-step GWAS to map the genomic regions behind both the direct and social components at once. In progress.

Master's research · University of Florida

Breed composition & GWAS for heat tolerance in Angus-Brahman cattle

Before swine, I worked on beef-cattle thermotolerance with Dr. Raluca Mateescu — mapping how breed composition and genetics shape the skin and epidermis properties that help cattle withstand heat stress.
